# Innerbloom Product Truth Map v1

## Purpose
Canonical source of truth for how Innerbloom works today, what each module proves, and which claims are misleading.

## Core promise
**See your progress, adjust your challenge, and build habits sustainably.**

Innerbloom helps people continue through low- and high-energy periods. Minimum viable action still counts; stronger periods can justify greater challenge.

## Product journey
1. Create an account, including Google sign-in.
2. Choose a weekly rhythm from 1 to 4.
3. Review and edit starter tasks.
4. Enter the dashboard.
5. Complete the first Daily Quest.

## Rhythm
The rhythm is the intended weekly frequency for each task.

Truths:
- selected during onboarding;
- manually editable later;
- the system may recommend increasing it after a monthly global review;
- the recommendation is optional and never applied automatically;
- completing a task 5–7 times can mean exceeding the target or entering a streak.

Never claim:
- rhythm is reselected daily or every Monday;
- Innerbloom changes it automatically in real time;
- a missed week resets progress.

## Daily Quest
A daily retrospective about the previous day. The user reviews existing tasks, marks completed tasks, leaves incomplete tasks unmarked, and records the predominant emotion.

It is not a new daily plan, a random task generator, or a prediction engine.

## Emotion Chart
A calendar-like history of predominant emotions.

Supports:
- emotional pattern recognition;
- understanding that one bad day does not define a month;
- identifying frequent emotions;
- self-awareness.

Does not prove:
- adaptive difficulty;
- automatic rhythm changes;
- task progress;
- mental-health measurement.

## Growth Calibration
A monthly task-level recalibration based on actual completion.

- easy: 1 Growth Point;
- medium: 3 Growth Points;
- hard: 7 Growth Points.

Above-threshold performance may lower difficulty because the task represents less challenge. Below-threshold performance may raise difficulty because it still represents greater challenge. Growth Points represent calibrated challenge, not worth or moral effort.

The user can force difficulty by editing a task. If the task has more than one month of history, habit tracking resets and the edited task is treated as new.

Never claim Daily Energy directly determines difficulty or that calibration happens weekly or in real time.

## Habit achieved
After three consecutive months meeting the configured conditions, a task can unlock a permanent, shareable habit-achieved badge.

The user may continue tracking it or archive it. If tracking continues, it remains easy and worth 1 Growth Point.

Do not claim this scientifically guarantees permanent behaviour change.

## Daily Energy
A visual representation of today's accumulated momentum across Body, Mind, and Soul, based on recent actions and decay over time.

- Body maps to HP.
- Mind maps to Focus.
- Soul maps to Mood.
- It shows the current value plus a seven-day trend.
- It does not reset at midnight.
- Each pillar can decay at a different rate.

Supports:
- “See where your momentum is building.”
- “Small actions keep momentum alive.”
- “Recent choices shape how you begin today.”
- “See balance across Body, Mind, and Soul.”

Never claim it measures biological energy, medical health, cognitive capacity, or mental health, or that it automatically changes the plan.

## Progress
Progress is visible daily and weekly evidence of continued habit construction against the user’s selected rhythm, not perfection.

Best evidence:
- dashboard progress;
- task progress;
- weekly charts;
- pillar balance;
- habit-development history.

Bad pairings:
- streak-only UI for “progress without perfection”;
- rhythm selection for “no weekly reset”;
- Emotion Chart for task progression.

## Pillars
- **Body:** movement and physical activity.
- **Mind:** learning, development, hobbies, exploration.
- **Soul:** relationships, self-connection, rest, enjoyment, reflection, emotional life.

## Not current product behaviour
- weekly adaptive recalibration;
- real-time automatic task changes;
- emotion-driven automatic planning;
- automatic rhythm changes without consent.

## Mandatory truth check
Before approving a claim or visual:
1. What exact claim is being made?
2. Which current module proves it?
3. Does the screenshot directly support it?
4. Could a viewer infer a nonexistent capability?
5. Is it current rather than future behaviour?
